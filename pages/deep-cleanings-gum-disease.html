Deep Cleanings and Gum Disease Care - Clarenville, NL

When gums are inflamed or buildup extends below the gumline, a routine cleaning may not be enough

Deep cleanings and gum disease care are recommended when the concern is not just surface plaque, but inflammation, bleeding, pockets, or buildup that sits deeper around the teeth.

When It Comes Up

This kind of care is usually discussed when there are signs that gum disease is developing or has progressed beyond a regular hygiene visit

Patients may notice bleeding gums, tenderness, bad breath, visible buildup, or the sense that things have been worsening slowly over time. In other cases, the concern becomes clear during an exam and cleaning visit when the gums and supporting tissues are evaluated more closely.

  • Often recommended when gums bleed or stay inflamed
  • Addresses buildup below the gumline
  • Part of managing gum disease before it worsens
  • Usually paired with follow-up maintenance and home care guidance

Ongoing Care

Deep cleaning is usually the start of a gum-health plan, not the end of it

Gum disease care is most effective when the initial treatment is followed by regular monitoring, maintenance visits, and home-care changes that keep inflammation from building back up. The goal is stability: healthier gums, less bleeding, and fewer long-term problems around the teeth that are still healthy enough to keep.
