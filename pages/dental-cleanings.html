What to Expect

What a professional dental cleaning includes

A professional cleaning goes beyond what your toothbrush can reach. Your hygienist removes hardened tartar from tooth surfaces and along the gumline, polishes away surface staining, and reviews areas that may need more attention at home.

Most patients benefit from a cleaning every six months. Patients with gum disease, higher plaque buildup, or a history of cavities may benefit from more frequent visits — your hygienist will recommend the right schedule for you.

  • Scaling to remove plaque and hardened tartar
  • Polishing to clear surface staining
  • Gumline assessment and oral health review
  • Guidance on home care and any areas of concern

Why It Matters

Why regular cleanings are worth keeping up with

Tartar that builds up on tooth surfaces and below the gumline cannot be removed with a toothbrush. Left in place, it causes gum inflammation, bone loss, and eventually tooth loss. Regular professional cleanings interrupt that process before it becomes serious.

Cleanings are also when early signs of cavities, gum disease, or other concerns are most likely to be caught — when they are still easy and inexpensive to address.

Most dental problems are easier — and less expensive — to treat when they are caught early.

A cleaning every six months is one of the most effective things you can do for your long-term oral health. It removes what home care cannot, keeps your gums healthy, and gives your dentist the chance to spot small issues before they become bigger ones.
