# Live Site Audit

## Executive Summary
Clarenville Dental has a real local entity footprint, but the public site underuses it. The clinic already has the right business fundamentals for local search: a clear address, a phone-first conversion path, visible emergency positioning, and treatment categories that map to commercial demand. The problem is execution. The current public site looks thin, repetitive, and structurally weak for both rankings and bookings.

## Public Positioning Observed
- Brand used publicly: `Clarenville Dental Care`
- Address shown publicly: `259 Memorial Drive, Suite 201, Clarenville, NL A5A 1R4`
- Phone shown publicly: `(709) 200-0209`
- Search-visible email: `clarenville.dental@gmail.com`
- Public support/navigation pages visible in user prompt and search snippets: home, contact, appointment request, services, meet the team
- Publicly visible service themes: preventive dentistry, implant dentistry, dental aesthetics, Invisalign, wisdom teeth removal, emergency care

## Search-Visible Problems
- Homepage snippet shows repeated headings and duplicated section language.
- Appointment request page looks thin and low-trust.
- At least one service slug is misspelled: `/widsom-teeth-remvoal`.
- Search-visible copy is generic and does not separate service intent well.
- Public architecture appears to rely on broad descriptive pages instead of strong service landing pages.

## SEO Findings
- Homepage appears to carry too much topical weight.
- Commercial intent is under-separated.
- Thin content risk is high.
- Duplicate/near-duplicate heading risk is visible even from snippets.
- Metadata quality is likely inconsistent.
- Crawl/index structure is probably weak or under-managed.

## Local SEO Findings
- Clarenville relevance is naturally strong because the address is explicit.
- The site is not fully capitalizing on `Clarenville dentist`, `emergency dentist Clarenville`, `Invisalign Clarenville`, and `dental implants Clarenville`.
- Smaller-market strategy should stay exact, practical, and conversion-led rather than broad-city editorial.
- "Near me" support should be woven into emergency, cleaning, Invisalign, implants, and wisdom-teeth pathways, but not forced into standalone spam pages.

## CRO Findings
- Emergency path should be faster and more obvious.
- Phone-first conversion deserves more prominence.
- Appointment request UX likely creates friction instead of momentum.
- Trust proof is thin relative to the high-trust nature of dental decisions.
- New-patient reassurance is underdeveloped.

## Internal Linking Findings
- Weak hub-to-detail routing likely exists on the public site.
- Service-to-service linking appears shallow.
- Contact and appointment pages are not acting as strong conversion hubs.
- Team/trust pages do not appear to support commercial pages strongly enough.

## Competitor Overlap
Public search results show Elliott Dental Studio as the clearest visible competitor in Clarenville:
- stronger service-specific pages such as implants
- stronger contact/new-patient framing
- more explicit emergency messaging

Inference:
- Clarenville is small enough that page clarity, trust, and local specificity matter more than content volume.
- Clarenville Dental can compete by having cleaner service separation, faster emergency conversion, and better trust architecture.

## Immediate Audit Conclusions
- Keep Clarenville exact-local targeting.
- Build around emergency, Invisalign, implants, family/general, preventive/cleaning, and wisdom-teeth demand.
- Use support pages to reinforce trust and booking, not to inflate page count.
