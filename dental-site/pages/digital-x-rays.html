Digital X-Rays - Clarenville, NL

Digital dental X-rays help reveal what an exam cannot see on its own

X-rays are a practical part of preventive and diagnostic care. They help detect changes between the teeth, around roots, under old dental work, and in the surrounding bone.

Why They Are Used

Many dental problems start in places that are not visible during a normal visual check

Even with a careful exam, there are limits to what can be seen directly. Cavities can develop between teeth. Infection can form near the root. Bone levels can shift gradually. A tooth may crack or a filling may fail below the visible surface. Digital X-rays give the clinic more complete information so recommendations are based on evidence, not guesswork.

  • Finding decay between teeth
  • Checking roots, surrounding bone, and signs of infection
  • Reviewing developing wisdom teeth and eruption patterns
  • Supporting treatment planning for fillings, crowns, root canals, and extractions
  • Tracking changes over time as part of routine care

When They Matter

X-rays are often part of new patient exams, routine preventive care, and problem-focused visits

Not every visit needs the same imaging. The timing depends on your history, symptoms, existing dental work, and what the clinical exam suggests. For a new patient, updated images often help establish a reliable baseline. For an emergency visit, they can help pinpoint the cause of pain. For preventive care, they help catch changes before they become larger treatment needs.

That is why digital X-rays fit naturally into general dentistry rather than standing apart from it. They support clearer answers and more confident treatment decisions.
