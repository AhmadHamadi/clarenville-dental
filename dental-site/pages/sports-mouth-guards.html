Sports Mouth Guards - Clarenville, NL

Sports mouth guards help reduce the risk of broken teeth and dental injuries during play

For kids, teens, and adults who play contact sports or activities with a risk of impact, a mouth guard is one of the simplest ways to protect teeth before an accident happens.

Why They Matter

Dental injuries can happen quickly in sports, and prevention is easier than repair

Chipped teeth, broken teeth, knocked-out teeth, and soft-tissue injuries can all happen during sports or recreational play. A sports mouth guard is meant to lower that risk by adding a protective layer between the teeth and impact forces.

  • Helps protect teeth during contact and athletic activities
  • Useful for children, teens, and adults in sport
  • Supports prevention before an injury happens
  • Often part of broader family dental planning for active patients

Protection For Active Families

Sports guards fit naturally into family-focused preventive care

Families often think about checkups and cleanings, but sports protection matters too. For patients who play hockey, basketball, martial arts, football, or other impact-related sports, protecting the teeth is part of protecting long-term oral health.
